## F&Q
**What is your long-term goal?**

While many HCI researchers have contributed design heuristics for user-centered systems, such as Nielsen’s usability heuristics, we aim to do the same for learner-centered systems.
By designing and evaluating interactive tools in contexts like programming, data science, and collaborative problem solving, our goal is to distill a set of design heuristics that capture what makes learning tools effective, engaging, and cognitively meaningful. These heuristics can guide educators, researchers, and developers in creating systems that not only make tasks easier but also make learning meaningful.

**How do you achieve this long-term goal?**

We achieve this by speculating on how computing technologies can augment human intelligence in the near future. 
We use design-based research to build prototypes or design proxies that let us study these futures in the present. 
We work with some of the best engineers and designers—creative, forward-thinking students who turn speculative ideas into concrete systems that explore what learning could look like next.

We achieve this by empirically designing and evaluating interactive systems that support learning in real contexts. 
Through iterative prototyping, user studies, and classroom deployment, we identify patterns and principles that inform learner-centered design.

We achieve this by exchanging insights with like-minded collaborators, including educational developers, teachers, and researchers from a range of disciplines. We value open dialogue that bridges theory and practice, and we see collaboration as essential to designing impactful learning technologies.
