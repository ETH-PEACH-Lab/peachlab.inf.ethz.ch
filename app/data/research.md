In human–computer interaction, we often design tools that minimize unnecessary cognitive effort to maximize usability, enabling users to complete complex tasks quickly and confidently.
In learning contexts, however, whether in classrooms or the workplace, reducing cognitive effort too much can bypass the thinking that makes learning meaningful and create an **illusion of competence**.
Take debugging as an example: simply applying a fix, whether copied from Stack Overflow or suggested by an AI assistant, might make the code run, but it does not ensure the learner understands why the bug occurred, why the fix works, or how to prevent similar issues in the future.

At the PEACH Lab, we focus on addressing the gaps between completing a task (**doing**) and truly understanding the underlying concepts (**learning**).
We design interactive systems that help people engage deeply with technical concepts, make their reasoning visible, and connect what they are doing to what they are learning. 
Our work spans programming, data, algorithmic reasoning, and AI literacy, and extends to learning and productivity tools beyond programming.
