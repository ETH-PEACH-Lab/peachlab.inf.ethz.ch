### Group Project: A Tool for Learning AI Literacy (60%)

**Deadline:** W15 May 29, 11:59 CEST

**Submission:** A link to the project website

You will work in groups of **3–5** students.

In this project, you will design, build, and evaluate an educational tool that helps a specific audience understand an aspect of AI literacy. Your goal is to create something that could realistically be used in a classroom, workshop, or outreach setting.

Throughout the semester, we will schedule check-in points during exercise sessions to help groups stay on track and receive feedback on their problem definition, prototype, and evaluation plan. 
These check-ins are not graded; only the final submission will be assessed.
More detailed instructions about the check-ins will be provided on Moodle.

#### Project Overview
AI literacy is the ability to understand how AI systems work, use them effectively, critically evaluate their outputs, and make responsible decisions when AI influences learning, work, creativity, and everyday life.

AI is no longer a technology used only by experts. It is increasingly embedded in classrooms, creative tools, search engines, recommendation systems, and productivity platforms, influencing how people access information, solve problems, and create knowledge. As AI becomes part of everyday decision-making, AI literacy is a foundational skill that helps people remain critical, informed, and responsible users of these systems.

Your task is to build an educational tool that helps a clearly defined audience develop understanding of one aspect of AI literacy.

#### Core Tasks

1. **Define Target Audience**  
	- Choose a clearly defined user group (no "everyone").  
	- Justify why AI literacy matters for them.
2. **Define Learning Objectives**  
	- Focus on one or two specific AI literacy concepts, grounded in established AI literacy frameworks (e.g., https://ailiteracyframework.org/).  
	- Define clear, measurable learning goals.
3. **Ground in Literature**  
	- Identify related work (e.g., tools above or research papers).  
	- Explain how your project builds on or differs from prior approaches.
4. **Design & Build a Prototype**  
	- Create a deployable educational tool.  
	- Format is open (app, game, visualization, tangible toolkit, etc.).  
	- Must include a functioning core interaction that your audience can actually use.
5. **Evaluate with Real Users**  
	- Minimum **N ≥ 5** from the target audience.  
	- Assess learning outcomes (did people learn what you intended?).  
	- Assess usability and overall experience.

#### Deliverables

Your main deliverable is a **public project website** that presents:

- **About**: problem, target audience, learning objectives, design rationale, related work, team.  
- **Tool / Demo**: access to the tool, setup and usage instructions, screenshots or video, and clear AI disclosure (what AI is used, what it does, and its limitations).  
- **Resources**: lesson plan, worksheets, facilitation guide, and any setup instructions needed for educators to run your activity.  
- **Evaluation**: study setup (participants, procedure, measures), feedback, key observations, and evidence of effectiveness.

A good example of a complete project website: https://research.google/ai-quests/intl/en_gb/about
